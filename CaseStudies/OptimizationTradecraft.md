## âœ… What You Already Know (and Will Apply Here)

Everything you need to understand this paper conceptually has already been covered.

As you read, you should recognize ideas related to:

### Decision Statements

You've learned that prescriptive analytics starts with a decision, not data or math.

This paper repeatedly emphasizes:

- Writing the decision down in plain language
- Clarifying why the decision matters
- Making sure everyone agrees on what is being decided

### Objectives and Constraints

You already know that optimization models are built around:

- An **objective** (what we're trying to optimize)
- **Constraints** (real-world limits)

This paper discusses how:

- Poorly defined objectives lead to unusable results
- Missing or misunderstood constraints cause models to fail
- Business rules often matter more than mathematical elegance

### Interpretation and Communication

You've learned that optimization models produce recommendations, not commands.

This paper strongly reinforces:

- The importance of executive summaries
- Explaining results without math
- Presenting insights in a way decision-makers already understand
