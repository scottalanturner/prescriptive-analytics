# Final Project Rubric — ISYS 320U Prescriptive Analytics

This project is graded in two parts, assessed separately.

- **Part 1 — Written Deliverable (Notebook + Slides):** 100 points
- **Part 2 — Presentation:** 100 points = **10% of course grade**

---

## Part 1: Written Deliverable — 100 Points

| **Criterion (Weight)** | **Exemplary** | **Proficient** | **Developing** | **Insufficient** |
|------------------------|---------------|----------------|----------------|------------------|
| **1. Decision Framing and Analytics Context (20 pts)** | **20 pts** — Decision statement is crisp (1–2 sentences) and clearly identifies who decides, what is decided, and what success looks like. Problem is convincingly placed on the analytics continuum with a specific explanation of why prescriptive analytics applies rather than descriptive or predictive. Decision variables, objective, constraints, and tradeoffs are precisely defined and coherent. | **16 pts** — Decision statement and analytics context are mostly clear with minor gaps in specificity or precision. Analytics continuum placement is present but may be surface-level. Decision framing elements are defined but one or two have minor ambiguity or missing detail. | **12 pts** — Decision statement is vague or too broad to guide the model. Analytics continuum discussion is missing or generic. Decision variables, objective, or constraints are incomplete or poorly defined in ways that affect model quality. | **0 pts** — Decision framing is missing or so unclear the model cannot be evaluated in context. No meaningful engagement with why this is a prescriptive problem. |
| **2. Optimization Model — Structure and Solve (25 pts)** | **25 pts** — Model includes a correct linear objective, at least two meaningful constraints that reflect stated business rules, and at least one integer or binary decision variable that is justified. Model solves and status is confirmed. Output is interpreted correctly in plain language that a non-technical reader can follow. Infeasibility, if encountered, is diagnosed and resolved with a clear explanation of the tradeoff. | **20 pts** — Model is mostly correct with minor formulation issues that do not significantly change the recommendation. Integer or binary element is present. Solve output is shown and mostly interpreted correctly; plain-language interpretation may lack depth or precision. | **15 pts** — Model has notable gaps: missing or incorrect constraints, objective is poorly specified, or no integer/binary variable is present. Interpretation of results is incomplete, inaccurate, or too technical for a non-expert reader. | **0 pts** — Model is missing, non-functional, or does not demonstrate understanding of optimization formulation. No meaningful result or interpretation. |
| **3. Sensitivity Analysis and Time Dimension (25 pts)** | **25 pts** — Three parameters are tested at ±20% with clear results showing which changes the recommendation most. Student identifies the most critical parameter and explains what that means for decision confidence in plain language. Time dimension is substantive: either the model includes time-indexed variables, or the rollout/phasing is shown clearly, with explanation of how constraints or demand shift across periods. | **20 pts** — Sensitivity analysis covers three parameters with mostly correct results; identification of most critical parameter is present but may lack depth in plain-language explanation. Time dimension is addressed meaningfully, with minor gaps in specificity or connection to the model. | **15 pts** — Sensitivity analysis covers fewer than three parameters, or results are unclear or not connected to decision confidence. Time dimension is mentioned but not meaningfully demonstrated — a single sentence or generic statement does not meet the standard. | **0 pts** — Sensitivity analysis is missing or not executable. Time dimension is absent. |
| **4. Nonlinear Caveats and Final Recommendation (15 pts)** | **15 pts** — Identifies a specific, plausible nonlinear relationship in the domain and explains clearly what diminishing returns would look like and how that would change or qualify the recommendation. Final recommendation is concrete, specific, and actionable: names what to do, which assumption must be validated, and what the first step is. | **12 pts** — Nonlinear caveat is present and connected to the domain but may be generic or lack specific explanation of consequences. Final recommendation is mostly actionable but may be vague on assumptions or next steps. | **9 pts** — Nonlinear section is superficial or disconnected from the actual model. Final recommendation is present but vague ("the organization should consider…") or missing key components. | **0 pts** — Nonlinear caveats and/or final recommendation are missing. |
| **5. Professional Written Deliverable (15 pts)** | **15 pts** — Notebook runs cleanly top-to-bottom with no errors. Writing throughout is grammatically correct, professional, and manager-ready — no leftover `[TODO]` markers, placeholder text, or informal language. At least one visualization is present with a clear title and labeled axes. GitHub repo is accessible and includes a short README. Slides are submitted alongside the notebook (PDF or PowerPoint). | **12 pts** — Notebook runs with only minor issues. Writing is mostly professional, with occasional grammar or formatting problems. Visualization is present but may have minor labeling gaps. GitHub and slides are present with small omissions. | **9 pts** — Notebook has execution or formatting issues, or several `[TODO]` markers remain. Writing has noticeable quality problems or informal tone. Visualization is missing or unlabeled. GitHub setup is incomplete. | **0 pts** — Notebook is broken or largely incomplete. Writing quality is poor. Required assets (slides, GitHub) are missing or inaccessible. |

## Part 2: Presentation — 100 Points (= 10% of Course Grade)

| **Criterion (Weight)** | **Exemplary** | **Proficient** | **Developing** | **Insufficient** |
|------------------------|---------------|----------------|----------------|------------------|
| **1. Slide Quality and Visual Clarity (25 pts)** | **25 pts** — 5–8 slides that are clean, readable, and visually purposeful. Every slide has a clear headline that makes a claim (not just a label). Every chart has a title and labeled axes. No slide is overloaded with text. Slides read as a coherent story from open to close — someone who missed the verbal presentation could follow the argument from slides alone. | **20 pts** — Slides are generally clean and readable, with minor formatting or labeling gaps. Most headlines are informative. Story arc is mostly clear, with one or two slides that are harder to follow without the verbal explanation. | **15 pts** — Slides have notable problems: overloaded with text, missing chart labels, headlines that are vague labels rather than claims, or unclear flow between slides. Requires the verbal presentation to be understandable. | **0 pts** — Slides are missing, visually incoherent, or clearly unprepared. Notebook is shared instead of slides. |
| **2. Decision and Recommendation Communication (25 pts)** | **25 pts** — Opens with the problem and recommendation in terms a non-technical VP would immediately understand. Avoids jargon (no "objective function," "binary variable," or "feasible region" without plain-language translation). Recommendation on the closing slide is specific and actionable, not vague. The VP would know exactly what to approve or reject. | **20 pts** — Problem and recommendation are communicated clearly with only occasional technical language that is mostly self-corrected or explained. Closing recommendation is mostly specific, with minor vagueness in the action or caveats. | **15 pts** — Technical jargon is used without translation in ways that would lose a non-technical audience. Opening does not clearly frame the decision, or closing recommendation is too vague to act on. | **0 pts** — Presentation is incomprehensible to a non-technical audience, or no clear recommendation is made. |
| **3. Analytical Depth and Honest Caveats (25 pts)** | **25 pts** — Sensitivity findings, time dimension, and nonlinear caveats are represented on slides with clarity and proportion — not buried or skipped. Student shows genuine understanding of what the model can and cannot do. The "where this model has limits" slide reads as honest and thoughtful, not as a box-check. When presenting the analysis, student demonstrates they understand *why* results came out as they did, not just *what* they are. | **20 pts** — Key analytical elements (sensitivity, time, caveats) are present in the presentation with minor gaps in depth or clarity. Student demonstrates reasonable understanding of results. Caveat slide is present but may be generic. | **15 pts** — One or more analytical sections are skipped, superficial, or clearly misunderstood in the verbal presentation. Caveats feel like checkbox compliance rather than genuine engagement. | **0 pts** — Analytical depth is absent. Presentation is a surface-level summary with no engagement with sensitivity, time dimension, or model limitations. |
| **4. Delivery and Q&A (25 pts)** | **25 pts** — Presentation is delivered confidently within the 10-minute window, with consistent eye contact (camera) and a pace that suits the audience. Q&A responses are direct and honest — student defends their choices with reasoning, acknowledges what they do not know, and does not get flustered by challenge questions. Student stays in the role of analyst briefing a VP, not student presenting homework. | **20 pts** — Delivery is generally confident and within time, with minor pacing or eye contact issues. Q&A responses are mostly on-point, with occasional uncertainty handled reasonably. Role framing is mostly maintained. | **15 pts** — Delivery has notable problems: significantly over/under time, reading from slides, or visible unpreparedness. Q&A responses are evasive, inaccurate, or demonstrate that the student does not fully understand their own model. | **0 pts** — Presentation is not delivered, or delivery is so unprepared that no meaningful evaluation is possible. |
