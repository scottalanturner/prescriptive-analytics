| **1. Problem Framing & Binary Decisions (20 pts)** | **18–20 pts** — Domain and decision problem are described clearly and specifically (who decides, what is decided, why it matters). Defines 5–8 well-chosen binary decisions; each has a clear description and unambiguous meaning of 1 vs 0 that matches Lesson 8’s yes/no framing. | **15–17 pts** — Domain and decision problem are mostly clear, with minor gaps or vague wording. | **2. Policy IF–THEN Translation & Coverage (20 pts)** | **18–20 pts** — Provides 6–8 relevant policies tied to the chosen decision problem. For each, clearly identifies the IF (trigger) and THEN (required outcome) and rewrites it as a precise IF–THEN rule that could be encoded by someone else without guesswork. | **15–17 pts** — Provides at least 5 mostly relevant policies. IF and THEN parts are identified for most policies; IF–THEN rewrites are generally clear but may include minor ambiguity or wording that would need small clarification before encoding. | **10–14 pts** — Provides fewer than 5 policies or some are only loosely related to the decision problem. IF and THEN parts are missing or unclear for several policies; IF–THEN rewrites are vague or incomplete, making encoding difficult. | **0–9 pts** — Policies are missing, largely irrelevant to the decision problem, or not rewritten as IF–THEN rules. Little or no evidence of understanding how to translate policy language into conditional rules. |
| **3. Logical Constraint Typing & Policy Stack (25 pts)** | **23–25 pts** — Correctly classifies nearly all policies into Lesson 8 types (activation, suppression, either-or, mutually exclusive, complementary) and provides clear, accurate justifications for each choice, including discussion of any ambiguity. Builds a complete, readable policy stack (decisions × policies) and thoughtfully interprets which decisions are heavily vs lightly constrained and where constraints might be missing. | **19–22 pts** — Mostly correct classifications with only minor errors or omissions; justifications are present but may be brief. Policy stack includes most decisions and policies and is readable; interpretation identifies some heavily vs lightly constrained decisions but may miss subtler points. | **13–18 pts** — Multiple misclassifications of constraint types or missing justifications. Policy stack is incomplete (missing several decisions or policies) or hard to read; interpretation is superficial or largely descriptive without insight into constraint patterns. | **0–12 pts** — Little or no attempt to classify constraint types or classifications are largely incorrect. Policy stack is missing, unusable, or shows minimal understanding of how decisions and policies relate. |
| **4. Feasibility Reasoning, Conflicts, and Explanation (20 pts)** | **18–20 pts** — Clearly identifies at least one realistic pair or trio of policies that could conflict or create tension. Provides a concrete, domain-specific narrative of what “no feasible solution” would look like and thoughtfully explains which policies they would relax, reinterpret, or renegotiate first and why. Explains the situation in language that a non-technical stakeholder could understand and act on. | **15–17 pts** — Identifies at least one plausible conflict but may not fully flesh out the interaction. Describes infeasibility and possible policy adjustments in generally clear terms, with some domain-specific detail. Non-technical explanation is understandable but may lack polish or completeness. | **10–14 pts** — Mentions conflicts or infeasibility only in general terms, with limited connection to the specific policies in the assignment. Proposed policy adjustments are vague or not well justified. Explanation for non-technical stakeholders is brief, unclear, or highly technical. | **0–9 pts** — Does not identify meaningful conflicts or does not address infeasibility. Little or no discussion of how to respond or explain the situation to stakeholders. |
| **5. Professional Formatting, GitHub Setup, and Writing Quality (15 pts)** | **14–15 pts** — GitHub repository is correctly set up with the assignment notebook and a short README (including student name and brief domain description). Writing throughout is well organized, grammatically correct, and professional—appropriate to hand to a manager. | **11–13 pts** — GitHub repo and README are present but may have minor omissions (e.g., brief or generic description). Writing has occasional grammar or spelling issues but is still readable and mostly professional. | **7–10 pts** — GitHub setup is incomplete (e.g., missing README or unclear repo organization). Notebook has execution or formatting issues, or several `[TODO]` markers remain. Writing shows noticeable grammar/spelling problems or informal tone that would need editing before sharing. | **0–6 pts** — GitHub repo is missing, inaccessible, or clearly not updated for this assignment.
