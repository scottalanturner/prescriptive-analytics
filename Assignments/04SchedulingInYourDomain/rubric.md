## Assignment 04: Scheduling in Your Domain — Rubric


| **Criterion (Weight)** | **Exemplary** | **Proficient** | **Developing** | **Insufficient** |
|------------------------|---------------|----------------|----------------|------------------|
| **1. Problem Framing and Schedule Structure (20 pts)** | **20 pts** — Domain and scheduling decision are clearly defined (what is scheduled, by whom, across what time periods, and why). Time periods, resources, demand requirements, and objective are specific and coherent. Synthetic dataset has 6-8 periods with required columns and is well documented (including LLM-use sentence). | **16 pts** — Problem framing is mostly clear with minor ambiguity. Schedule structure elements are present but may have small gaps in specificity or consistency. Dataset generally meets size/column expectations with minor documentation issues. | **12 pts** — Problem statement or schedule structure is incomplete, vague, or weakly connected to a real decision context. Dataset is partially complete (e.g., wrong size or missing detail) or minimally documented. | **0 pts** — Problem framing is missing or unclear. Schedule structure is not usable for modeling, and/or required dataset elements are missing. |
| **2. PuLP Model Correctness and Solve (25 pts)** | **25 pts** — Correct time-indexed decision variables, coverage constraints by period, at least one resource/capacity limit, and an appropriate objective. Model solves and outputs are interpreted correctly. If infeasible, student clearly diagnoses and relaxes a constraint appropriately. | **20 pts** — Model is mostly correct with minor formulation issues that do not significantly affect interpretation. Solve process is shown and outputs are mostly interpreted correctly. Infeasibility handling is attempted if needed. | **15 pts** — Model has notable formulation errors (missing/incorrect constraints or objective) or weak interpretation. Solve output is incomplete, unclear, or partially incorrect. | **0 pts** — Model is missing, non-functional, or demonstrates minimal understanding of scheduling formulation and solve workflow. |
| **3. Visualization and Schedule Interpretation (20 pts)** | **20 pts** — Includes a clear, relevant schedule visualization (Gantt-style or coverage comparison) with proper title and labeled axes. Narrative interpretation connects the visual to operational meaning (coverage, bottlenecks, practicality) in clear plain language. | **16 pts** — Visualization is present and generally clear, with minor labeling or formatting gaps. Interpretation is understandable but somewhat limited in depth or practical insight. | **12 pts** — Visualization is weakly connected to the model, poorly labeled, or difficult to read. Interpretation is brief, generic, or partially inaccurate. | **0 pts** — Required visualization is missing or unusable, and interpretation is missing or not meaningful. |
| **4. Stakeholder Change and Tradeoff Explanation (20 pts)** | **20 pts** — Implements one valid stakeholder change, re-solves the model, and compares before/after results clearly. Cost-vs-service tradeoff is explained accurately in non-technical language suitable for a manager. Reflection demonstrates clear understanding of why results changed and why that matters. | **16 pts** — Stakeholder change and comparison are completed with minor issues in execution or explanation. Tradeoff explanation is mostly clear; reflection is present but may be less specific or polished. | **12 pts** — Stakeholder change is attempted but incomplete, weakly implemented, or weakly compared to base. Tradeoff explanation is vague or too technical. Reflection is minimal or partially off-target. | **0 pts** — Stakeholder change is missing or not meaningfully implemented. No clear tradeoff explanation or reflection. |
| **5. Professional Formatting and GitHub (15 pts)** | **15 pts** — Notebook is complete, well organized, and runs cleanly top-to-bottom. Writing is grammatically clean, professional, and manager-ready. GitHub repo is accessible and includes required assignment materials. | **12 pts** — Notebook is mostly complete and readable, with minor execution/formatting/writing issues. GitHub setup is present with small omissions. | **9 pts** — Notebook has multiple formatting or execution issues, and/or writing quality is inconsistent. GitHub setup is incomplete or unclear. | **0 pts** — Notebook is largely incomplete or broken, writing quality is poor, and/or GitHub submission is missing or inaccessible. |
